# Princeton GEO Tactics — 9 research-validated methods

Source: Aggarwal, Murahari, Rajpurohit, Kalyan, Narasimhan, Deshpande. *GEO: Generative Engine Optimization.* KDD 2024. arxiv:2311.09735.

Tested on 10,000 queries. Headline: +40% visibility average.

## The 9 methods + measured impact

| # | Method | Impact |
|---|--------|--------|
| 1 | Cite sources (emphasize external citations) | **+115% citation likelihood** |
| 2 | Add expert quotes (with attribution + quotation marks) | +41% citation likelihood |
| 3 | Add statistics (numeric claims with sources) | +40% visibility |
| 4 | Improve fluency (natural language, not formulaic) | +X% (varies by domain) |
| 5 | Authority signaling (credential markup, named contributors) | +X% |
| 6 | Easy-to-understand (Flesch-Kincaid grade 8-10) | +X% |
| 7 | Keyword stuffing | **NEGATIVE** (do not use) |
| 8 | Topic relevance (one primary topic per page) | +X% |
| 9 | Length optimization (appropriate to topic, not arbitrary minimum) | +X% |

## Domain-specific findings
Effects vary by domain — some methods compound multiplicatively in academic and B2B content but flatten in consumer e-commerce.

## Application in best-aeo-skill
Each method maps to a specific evidence collector + scoring weight in the Citability vector (35%).
